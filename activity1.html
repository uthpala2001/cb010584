...2.Hot air balloon rides...



.Dambulla - Kandalama's climate is ideal for ballooning due to its calm and predictable nature.

...4.Hiking...



. Hiking in Sri Lanka is something that most tourists have fantasized about at some point. The name alone conjures up images of lush, green rainforest, rolling mountains, tropical wildlife, and turquoise water. The best hiking in Sri Lanka exemplifies this.


. Lonely Planet named the country the "best country in the world" for travelers to visit in 2019. They praised its "rich and accessible wildlife," as well as the surf scene, culture, and friendliness.





...HIKE THROUGH THE KNUCKLES MOUNTAIN RANGE...



.Much of the hiking in Sri Lanka takes place on well-worn paths and routes. No, not in the Knuckles mountain range. This is the route we take in Sri Lanka on our own adventure, taking a couple of nights to get through the Knuckles. Ruvi, an experienced local guide, told us that "even Sri Lankans don't really know the trail that we're hiking on," and that when he showed the photos to other guides after returning from the route, they all wanted to go visit.



. The trail leads you deep into the rainforest as well as the rugged wilderness of the Knuckles Range. The name comes from the range's resemblance to a clenched fist. Consider yourself surrounded by majestic mountains that reach heights of up to 2000 meters. Massive cascading waterfalls dot the landscape.


.You pass through small villages and spend the night wild camping in the middle of nowhere. You'll almost certainly see buffalos, and if you're lucky, you might also see boars, toque macaques, and purple-faced monkeys. If you're really lucky, you might even see elephants and leopards in the jungle. Though the chances of running into them while hiking with their backpacks are slim.
